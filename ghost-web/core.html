core

Ghost Core.

A fork with a finite bootstrap. The treasury collects 0.5% of every block subsidy until it reaches 21 BTC, then decays over five years to zero. After that, every satoshi of pool fee goes to the node operators. Ghost runs forever on its own.

phase · bootstrap

Bootstrap → Decentralising → Sovereign.

Three phases. One direction. No governance vote needed — the transition is driven mechanically by the treasury balance and a fixed calendar.

  1. Bootstrap
    Treasury fills to 21 BTC at 0.5% per block. Pool fee split 50/50 between treasury and node reward pool.
  2. Decentralising
    Threshold reached. Treasury's share of each pool fee steps down by 10 percentage points per year over five years.
  3. Sovereign
    Decay complete. 100% of every pool fee goes to the node reward pool. Ghost is fully operator-owned.

the mesh

Nodes keeping Ghost alive.

The mesh is the pool. Every node can propose payouts, validate blocks, vote in BFT consensus. No distinction is drawn between clearnet and Tor nodes — operators choose how to run, the mesh counts them the same way. This page reports only aggregates so individual operators stay invisible.

principles

What stays true in every phase.

  • No pre-mine
    Every satoshi enters circulation via a mined block. No founder allocation, no insider grant.
  • No foundation, no company
    Open source under MIT. The protocol is the protocol. No entity can change the rules unilaterally.
  • Non-custodial by design
    The pool never holds miner funds. Every payout is an on-chain coinbase output direct to the miner's address.
  • Run your own node
    A home server, pc or laptop running Bitcoin Ghost can be a Ghost node. No whitelisting, no permission, no minimum stake.
  • Operator-defined policy
    Every node sets its own mempool policy. There is no canonical "Ghost policy" — the mesh doesn't care what transactions you include.
  • Treasury decays to zero
    The bootstrap mechanism is designed to extinguish itself. After five years of decay, 100% of the pool fee goes to node operators.
