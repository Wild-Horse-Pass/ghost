What Is GhostTap

GhostTap is a cross-platform Bitcoin wallet and merchant payment terminal. Send and receive private Bitcoin payments with a tap of your phone or a scan of a QR code.

Available on iOS, Android, and desktop (macOS, Windows, Linux via Tauri v2). NFC tap-to-pay for in-person payments, with full Ghost Pay L2 integration for instant transfers.

// 02

Features

Built for privacy-first Bitcoin payments, from daily spending to merchant point-of-sale.

🔒
Privacy-First
BIP-352 Silent Payments for unlinkable receive addresses. No address reuse, no tracking.
⚡
Instant Transfers
Ghost Pay L2 for ~10 second confirmations. No channels, no liquidity management.
📲
NFC + QR Payments
Tap your phone to pay at merchants, or scan QR codes. Works with standard NFC terminals.
🏪
Merchant Mode
Invoices, receipts, and CSV export. Turn any device into a Bitcoin point-of-sale terminal.
💾
Local-First Storage
SQLite database with keychain-backed encryption. Your keys stay on your device.
📴
Offline-Capable
Generate receive addresses and prepare transactions offline. Sync when you're back online.
// 03

How It Works

GhostTap connects to the Ghost network through a Ghost Service Provider (GSP) for chain sync, while keeping all keys and signing local to your device.

1

Key Derivation

Standard BIP-44 HD key derivation (coin type 531). Create a new wallet or import via BIP-39 seed phrase. Keys are stored in your device's secure enclave or keychain.

2

Chain Sync via GSP

Connects to a Ghost Service Provider over WebSocket for lightweight chain synchronization. No need to download the full blockchain.

3

Ghost Pay L2

Instant transfers settle in ~10 seconds on the Ghost Pay L2 layer. Lock funds to Ghost Pay for fast spending, settle back to Bitcoin L1 on-demand.

4

L1 Settlement

Settle your L2 balance back to any Bitcoin address at any time. Your funds are always backed by real Bitcoin UTXOs.

No Node Required
GhostTap is a light wallet — it connects to the Ghost network through a GSP, so you can send and receive payments without running a full node.
